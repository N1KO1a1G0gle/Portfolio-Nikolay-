Notes on shooting in low light without a tripod

cover photograph

Most of the shots I care about happen after the light is already gone — a kitchen window at 9pm, a street lamp catching rain. No tripod, no time to set one up anyway.

What's helped more than any piece of gear is slowing down my own hands: bracing against a doorframe, exhaling before the shutter, shooting three frames instead of one and keeping the steadiest.

inline photograph

The grain that comes with pushing ISO isn't a flaw to fix later — on the right subject it reads as texture, almost like the photo remembers how dark the room actually was.

None of this is a substitute for a tripod when you have one. It's just what I've learned about working without.
