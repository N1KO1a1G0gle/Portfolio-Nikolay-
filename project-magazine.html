Editorial design — 2024

Redesigning the school magazine from the grid up

A forty-year-old student publication, rebuilt on a stricter grid and a type system its writing deserved.

Role
Art direction & layout
Timeline
8 weeks
Tools
InDesign, Photoshop
cover image

The problem

Four decades of hand-me-down templates had drifted into chaos: every spread set differently, headlines fighting body copy, photos cropped to fill gaps rather than to say anything. The writing was often good; the pages made it look accidental.

before — spread audit wall

The approach

I rebuilt the magazine on a twelve-column grid with exactly two typefaces and three permitted layouts per section — restrictive on purpose, because the next editors won't be designers. Photography got the same treatment: full column widths only, no wraps, captions in one consistent place.

spread — feature opener
spread — photo essay

The result

The redesigned issue was the first in years to run out of print copies. More importantly, the template shipped with a short documented rulebook, so the system survives the person who made it — which was always the real brief.
