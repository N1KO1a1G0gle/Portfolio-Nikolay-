Why I still sketch on paper before opening Figma

cover photograph

Every project still starts in the same two-euro notebook. Not because paper is romantic, but because it's fast and non-precious — a bad idea drawn in pen costs me eight seconds, and abandoning it costs nothing at all.

Figma makes everything look finished too early. A grey rectangle with rounded corners already looks like a decision, and once something looks like a decision, I start defending it instead of questioning it.

notebook spreads — thumbnail sketches

On paper, ten layouts fit on one spread, and the weakest ones die quietly in the margins. The sketch that survives usually isn't the prettiest — it's the one I kept redrawing without noticing.

By the time I open Figma, the thinking is mostly done, and the tool goes back to being what it's best at: precision. Paper decides what the thing is; pixels decide exactly how it looks.
