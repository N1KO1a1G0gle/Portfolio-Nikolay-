A year of small design decisions, reviewed

cover photograph

At the end of the year I scrolled back through twelve months of files, expecting to find a few big turning points. There weren't any. What I found instead was a pile of small decisions — most under a minute each — that quietly determined how everything turned out.

Cutting a navigation item nobody could justify. Choosing the boring typeface over the clever one, twice. Moving a button eight pixels and refusing to move it back. None of these felt like design at the time; they felt like tidying.

desk — year in files

The decisions I regret went the other way: the ones where I knew something was slightly off and shipped it anyway, telling myself it was too small to matter. Small compounds. Slightly-off compounds faster.

So that's the resolution, if it counts as one: good work turned out to be a long series of small, boring decisions, made carefully. The trick is treating them as if they're worth making well — because apparently they're the whole job.
