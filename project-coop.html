Brand & identity — 2025

A visual language for a slow-food cooperative

Type, packaging, and signage rooted in the texture of the region's paper mills.

Role
Identity design
Timeline
3 months
Tools
Illustrator, InDesign
cover image

The brief

Eleven small farms and one bakery, selling together at markets under a name nobody could quite draw. They wanted an identity that felt like the region without looking like every "artisanal" template — no kraft paper clichés, no hand-drawn wheat.

identity sheet — wordmark & type

The approach

The starting point came from a visit to the paper mill two villages over: the deckled edges, the watermarks, the way nothing lined up perfectly but everything belonged. The identity became type-led — a single serif set wide, printed on the mill's own stock, with one earth tone assigned to each farm so a market stall reads as a family rather than a franchise.

packaging — jars & bread bags
market signage

The result

The cooperative rolled the system out across packaging and signage without raising print costs — the mill stock was already local and cheap. Regulars started calling the stalls "the paper ones," which is about the best a designer can hope for.
